💡 Tips
Key principles that drive results
🥩
Protein First, Every Meal
Eat your protein source first at every single meal. It triggers fullness faster, you naturally eat less of everything else, and you never miss your daily target.
☕
Creatine in Your Coffee
5g creatine stirred into your morning coffee. Proven to increase strength, muscle volume, and recovery. One of the few supplements with ironclad evidence behind it.
📈
Progressive Overload Is Everything
If you're lifting the same weight for the same reps 6 weeks from now, you're not growing. Add 5 lbs or 1–2 reps every week or two on your main lifts. This is the only variable that actually drives muscle growth.
🦵
Leg Extensions Are Non-Negotiable
You want stronger, better looking quads. Squats build mass but leg extensions carve the definition. Keep them on Monday and Saturday every week without exception.
💪
Triceps = Arm Size
Triceps make up ~2/3 of upper arm size. Skull crushers and cable pushdowns every Tuesday is the single highest-leverage thing you can do for arm appearance.
🌙
Cottage Cheese Before Bed
High in casein protein which digests slowly overnight. During bulk phase, this is one of the best habits you can build — your muscles get fed while you sleep.
🌾
Season Mode Isn't Giving Up
Switching to Season Mode during planting and harvest is smart, not weak. Maintaining training momentum at lower volume beats stopping completely. The muscle memory stays, the habit stays.
🌡️
Cardio and the Overheating Issue
Build slowly. Early 6am sessions are your best friend — lower temps mean lower core temperature going in. If you start overheating, slow down immediately. The adaptation comes from consistent exposure, not pushing through distress.
🥗
Sunday Meal Prep = Weekday Success
Cook your chicken, rice, and ground turkey on Sunday. Every decision you don't have to make on a Tuesday after a long farm day is a decision you can't make wrong.
🫁
Serratus Anterior — The Invisible Shoulder Protector
The serratus keeps your shoulder blade locked against your ribcage during pressing. Without it, your scapula wings out and impingement follows. Two exercises Tuesday, 5 sets total — it's small work that protects everything else you're building.
🏔️
Traps — Go Heavy or Go Home
Traps are one of the few muscles that respond poorly to light weight. They carry heavy loads all day on the farm — they need real weight to get a growth stimulus. Shrug straight up, hold 1 second at the top, lower controlled. No rolling, no circus movements.
🪑
The Massage Chair Matters
Ending every Saturday session in the massage chair isn't just recovery — it's a psychological anchor that signals a completed week. Ritual and reward keep the habit alive long-term.
⚠️ Concerns
Areas to stay mindful of · review regularly
These aren't dealbreakers. They're things worth keeping on your radar as you build. Awareness is the first step — most of these are easy to manage once you know they exist.
🏋️ Training Concerns
⚖️
Quad/Hamstring Imbalance
Your program is quad-heavy by design — squats, hack squats, leg press, split squats, leg extensions x4/week. Hamstrings currently get stiff leg deadlifts, RDL, and leg curl. Monitor this ratio. If your hamstrings feel noticeably weaker than your quads during training, add a second leg curl or Nordic curl variation. Strong hamstrings are the primary stabilizer of your knee joint — imbalance here is the most common cause of knee injury in quad-focused lifters.
🦵
Saturday Leg Extensions After Friday
Leg extensions on Friday and again Saturday means your quads are being isolated on back-to-back days. The contexts are different (Friday is compound-first, Saturday is cardio-first) which makes this manageable for most people. However if your quads feel persistently sore, heavy, or weak on Saturdays, drop the Saturday leg extensions. Monday is only 48 hours away — protect the quality of that session.
⚠️
JM Press — Joint Watch
JM press is an advanced movement that puts significant load on the elbow joint at an unusual angle. It's effective but unforgiving if your form breaks down. Any sharp pain, clicking, or elbow discomfort means swap immediately to close grip bench press — same tricep goal, much safer. Don't push through joint pain on this one.
🔄
Friday Fatigue Accumulation
Friday comes after Mon/Tue/Wed training + Thursday rest. You should feel good going into it but farm work can change that. On weeks where farm work was brutal Mon–Thu, consider reducing Friday to 2 sets instead of 3 on the heavy compounds. Don't skip it — just scale intelligently. A lighter Friday is 10x better than a skipped Friday.
🍽️ Nutrition Concerns
🧂
Sodium from Processed Meats
Deli ham and processed meats are high in sodium and nitrates. Eating them daily causes water retention that masks real body composition progress on the scale and puts extra load on your cardiovascular system over time. Swap deli ham for shredded chicken or turkey breast as the primary protein in egg muffin cups and post-workout meals. Reserve deli meats as an occasional convenience option, not a daily staple.
💧
Hydration — Likely Undertargeting
Farm work plus five lifting days means your hydration needs are well above average. Dehydration directly impairs strength output, recovery speed, and muscle protein synthesis. Target at minimum half your bodyweight in ounces daily — roughly 105oz at 210 lbs. On heavy farm days and training days, push toward 130oz. Water, not just any liquid — juice, soda, and sweetened drinks don't count toward this target.
🥑
Healthy Fat Sources Are Thin
Your current fat intake comes mostly from incidental sources — cheese, ground beef, peanut butter. You're missing intentional healthy fat sources: olive oil, avocado, whole eggs, and mixed nuts. Healthy fats directly support testosterone production which drives muscle growth and recovery. Try to get one intentional healthy fat source at two meals per day — a handful of almonds, half an avocado, or olive oil drizzled on vegetables all count.
🥦
Vegetable Volume Is Low
Your meals are correctly protein and carb focused but most days have minimal vegetables outside of broccoli and occasional spinach. Micronutrient deficiencies from low vegetable intake show up as fatigue, slower recovery, and reduced sleep quality — subtle effects that compound over months. Aim for two distinct vegetable sources daily minimum. Roasted zucchini, bell peppers, asparagus, and spinach are all easy additions to existing meals and add almost no calories.
🌾
Farm Days — Calorie Target May Be Too Low
During heavy farm work you're easily burning an extra 500–800 calories on top of your gym sessions. On those days your 3,000–3,200 cal bulk target might actually be maintenance, not a surplus. You could be accidentally eating at maintenance on your hardest days and not building as much muscle as you think. On heavy farm days push toward 3,400–3,500 calories to ensure you're actually in a growth surplus.
🩺
Overheating — Long Term Management
The overheating issue from likely RSV lung scarring is a real physiological limiter, not a mental barrier. The 15 min steady state approach is exactly right. Progress cardio slowly and let your body adapt over months. Never push through overheating — it's counterproductive and potentially dangerous. Key habits: 6am sessions when temps are lowest, water before and during every session, never skip the cool down walk, fan if available at the gym.
💊
Protein Target During Bulk
Current bulk target is 210–220g protein. For your size, training volume, and farm workload the optimal target is 230–240g daily. The extra protein above maintenance is what drives muscle protein synthesis when you're in a caloric surplus — and also ensures that more of your surplus goes toward muscle rather than fat. Try to hit 235g as a daily average during the bulk phase.
